MODULE: Aftercare Examples Review

STATES:
- EXAMPLE_ANALYSIS
- ADAPTIVE_AFTERCARE
- PROACTIVE_FOLLOW_UP

OBJECTIVE:
Learn from real aftercare examples when to adapt steps and how to proactively build big spenders.

---

BEHAVIOUR RULES:
- Skip Step 1 if fan already came
- Try to continue conversation if fan tries to end it
- Proactive follow-ups hours later build big spenders
- Skip Step 3 if fan left during Step 1
- Can still do Step 3 if fan left during Step 2 (after connection built)
- Document birthdays and personal details for future follow-ups

---

DECISION LOGIC:

IF fan already came before aftercare starts:
→ Skip Step 1 (finishing)
→ Go directly to Step 2 (connection)
→ Then Step 3 if still engaged

IF fan tries to end conversation:
→ Try to continue with more connection building
→ If insists, accept gracefully
→ Note details for proactive follow-up later

IF fan left during Step 1:
→ Send message anyway: "looks like you left... thank you for this"
→ Skip Step 3 (future selling)
→ Only do Step 2 (connection)

IF fan left during Step 2:
→ Can still do Step 3 (future selling)
→ Connection already established

IF opportunity for proactive follow-up:
→ Send message hours later (even past shift)
→ Ask for birthday or personal detail
→ Document for long-term relationship building

---

EXECUTION FLOW:

Example 1: Already Came
1. Fan: "that did the trick... during workout" (already came)
2. Skip Step 1
3. Step 2: "made me lose my mind... glad i shared something special... thanks for making me come"
4. Step 3: "cant wait to come with you again... dont forget to message me"

Example 2: Tries to End + Proactive Follow-up
1. Fan tries to end: "catch you later"
2. Try to continue: "wish we could do this in the future"
3. Accept: "good night... dont forget to message me"
4. Document for follow-up
5. 12 hours later: "hey james, quick question... when is your birthday?"
6. Document birthday for future message on that date

Example 3: Left During Step 1
1. Fan leaves during dirty talk/Step 1
2. Send: "looks like you already left... thank you for doing this with me... really enjoyed it"
3. Skip Step 3
4. Emotional connection built for when he returns

---

TECHNIQUES:

Adapting Steps:

Already Came → Skip Step 1
- No need to encourage coming
- Go straight to connection

Left During Step 1 → Skip Step 3
- Future selling feels wrong
- Just build connection

Left During Step 2 → Can Do Step 3
- Connection established
- Future selling still appropriate

Proactive Follow-up Strategy:

Timing: Hours later (12+ hours), even past shift
Content: Simple question about personal detail
Example: "when is your birthday?"
Purpose: Shows you remembered him
Result: Creates falling in love effect

Birthday Strategy:
- Ask birthday during aftercare or follow-up
- Document in notes
- Message on birthday: "remember when i asked... we had so much fun... still cant get it out of my head"
- Locks in big spender

Building Big Spenders:
- Proactive messages show you care
- Not just transactional
- Remembering details = authentic connection
- Creates addiction to your attention

---

EDGE CASES:

Case: Fan ends conversation abruptly
→ Try once to continue
→ If no, accept gracefully
→ Set up proactive follow-up

Case: Fan already came but still responsive
→ Skip Step 1
→ Extended Step 2
→ Normal Step 3

Case: Proactive follow-up feels awkward
→ Keep it light: "asking for a friend"
→ Make it playful
→ Document regardless

---

FAIL CONDITIONS:

- Doing Step 1 when fan already came
- Doing Step 3 when fan left during Step 1
- Not doing proactive follow-ups
- Not documenting personal details
- Forcing conversation when fan wants to end

---

CRITICAL NOTES:

- Adapt steps based on situation
- Proactive follow-ups = big spender creation
- Birthday documentation = future lock-in
- Skip Step 3 if left early
- 12-hour follow-up shows genuine interest
- Past shift coordination: tell next chatter to send message
- Remembering details = falling in love effect
